## Design Context

### Users
**Primary**: Both students (elementary/middle school kids) and parents, in equal measure. Kids use the app independently to practice math. Parents monitor progress and configure their child's account. The design must work for a 9-year-old doing multiplication drills AND a parent scanning a progress dashboard — often on the same device, in the same session.

### Brand Personality
**Confident & structured** — trustworthy, organized, gives parents confidence in their child's learning. Not clinical or sterile, but reliable and warm. Think: a great tutor who is also a credentialed professional. Both children and parents should feel safe.

### Aesthetic Direction
- Current visual direction leans playful (Boogaloo, bouncing logo) but the brand intent is *confident & structured*
- There is a tonal mismatch to resolve: fun enough for kids, trustworthy enough for parents
- Should NOT look like: every other EdTech pastel app, generic school software, clinical LMS tools
- Theme: light mode primary (bright learning environment), with dark mode support

### Design Principles
1. **Dual-audience clarity** — every screen must feel appropriate for both a child and a parent. Not childish, not corporate.
2. **Trust through structure** — clear hierarchy, organized information, predictable patterns build parent confidence
3. **Delight without distraction** — personality comes through in moments (animations, rewards, branding) not in the base chrome
4. **Distinctive, not just different** — the identity should be genuinely memorable, not just "not generic"
5. **Accessible by default** — the built-in accessibility controls signal that this app takes all learners seriously
